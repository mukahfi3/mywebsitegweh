Thougher Than a Mountain

Garou stands with his back to the world he has always challenged. His back is covered in wounds, yet his steps remain firm, as if every battle is just a reminder that he is far from finished. In front of him, the world keeps moving, indifferent to who falls or rises. Behind him, the past watches coldly.

There are no cheers, no praise. Only the heavy breath of a man who refuses to bow down. Garou is not a hero, nor a monster. He is simply someone who chooses his own path, even when the world insists it is wrong.
